# Data Biz website seed pack

## 1. Purpose

This seed pack is for a fast POC website for **Data Biz**: a data and AI consultancy brand built from an existing Substack/media presence into a conversion-oriented consulting site.

The website POC should validate whether Data Biz can convert content-driven attention into:
- Discovery calls.
- Consultancy leads.
- Paid content interest.
- Stronger executive-facing positioning.

The site should not try to be a full corporate website yet. It should act as a sharp, senior, high-credibility landing site.

## 2. Core business goals

### Primary goals
- Establish thought leadership for employment, consulting, and future business building.
- Create a serious conversion point beyond Substack paid posts.
- Turn traffic from YouTube, Shorts, LinkedIn, and Substack into commercial intent.

### Secondary goals
- Test packaging of consulting offers.
- Test resonance of Data Biz as a consultancy brand.
- Build an asset that can later expand into a full services website.

## 3. What success means

The POC is successful if it helps answer these questions:
- Do visitors understand what Data Biz does within 5 seconds?
- Do they click into discovery calls?
- Which offer gets the most interest?
- Does the brand feel senior and credible enough for executives and data leaders?
- Does this work better than relying only on Substack paid content?

## 4. Audience

### Primary audience
- Executives: COO, CFO, CEO, CDO, VP Data, Head of Data.
- Senior data leaders managing org design, prioritization, stakeholder alignment, and AI strategy.

### Secondary audience
- Senior ICs in analytics engineering, BI, data product, and data science aspiring toward leadership.

### Likely buying situations
- Too many dashboards, no decision clarity.
- Too many “critical” assets, weak prioritization.
- Data org misalignment with business needs.
- AI pressure from leadership without a grounded operating model.
- Need for short, high-leverage advisory work instead of a full-time hire.

## 5. Brand positioning

### Brand statement
Data Biz helps executives and data leaders turn data and AI into operating leverage.

### Expanded positioning
Data Biz focuses on the operating decisions behind data and AI: KPI design, prioritization, dashboard decision systems, org shape, stakeholder alignment, and pragmatic AI adoption.

### What it is not
- Not a generic AI automation agency.
- Not a pure technical implementation shop.
- Not a dashboard factory.
- Not a low-end freelancer portfolio.

### Brand personality
- Sharp.
- Senior.
- Practical.
- Blunt.
- Operator-led.
- Low-BS.
- Strategic, but grounded in execution.

## 6. Messaging pillars

### Pillar 1: Decisions over dashboards
The core promise is not “better analytics.” It is better decisions, better prioritization, and clearer operating leverage.

### Pillar 2: Leadership over tooling
The message should avoid looking like a tools consultancy. Tooling only matters when tied to org design, business value, and speed-to-decision.

### Pillar 3: Pragmatic AI
AI should be framed as a leadership and operating-model decision, not just an experimentation gimmick.

### Pillar 4: Senior operator credibility
The site should feel like it is run by someone who has seen scaling pain in real companies, not someone repackaging frameworks from books.

## 7. Site role in the funnel

### Entry points
- YouTube long-form videos.
- YouTube Shorts.
- LinkedIn posts.
- Substack essays.
- Direct referrals.

### Core job of the website
The site is the main conversion layer between thought leadership and commercial action.

### Main conversion types

- Join email or Substack list.
- Explore consulting offers.
- Download a lead magnet later.

### Conversion priority order
1. Discovery calls.
2. Consulting inquiries.

## 8. Recommended POC scope

- Strong homepage narrative.

- No full case study library yet.

### Why one page
One page is enough to test messaging, credibility, and CTA behavior without slowing down execution.

## 12. Offer design principles

Each offer should be:
- Easy to understand.
- Framed around a business problem.
- Short in duration.
- Concrete in output.
- Senior enough to justify premium pricing later.

Avoid generic labels like “Consulting Services” or “Advisory Support.”

## 13. Offer seeds

### Offer 1: KPI Tree and Metrics Alignment Workshop
**Problem:** Leadership teams have dashboards and KPIs, but not a clean logic chain from company goals to team actions.

**Who it is for:** CDOs, Heads of Data, product and ops leadership teams, executive teams needing clearer performance logic.

**Format:** Half-day or full-day workshop.

**Outputs:**
- KPI tree draft.
- Metric definitions.
- Executive alignment notes.
- Recommended dashboard layers.

**Outcome statement:**
Create a shared metric model leadership can actually use for decisions.

### Offer 2: P0 Data Asset Prioritization Sprint
**Problem:** Everything is labeled critical, which destroys prioritization, trust, and response discipline.

**Who it is for:** Data platforms, analytics, BI, ops, and engineering leaders in scaling companies.

**Format:** 1–2 week sprint.

**Outputs:**
- Critical asset framework.
- Priority tiers.
- Service level expectations.
- Incident ownership map.

**Outcome statement:**
Move from noisy “everything matters” chaos to a defendable criticality model.

### Offer 3: Data & AI Leadership Advisory
**Problem:** Leadership teams need senior guidance on data orgs, operating models, stakeholder alignment, and AI choices, but do not yet need a full-time senior hire.

**Who it is for:** CEOs, COOs, CFOs, CDOs, VP Data, startup and scale-up leadership teams.

**Format:** Ongoing advisory, monthly or 6-week intensive.

**Outputs:**
- Leadership advisory sessions.
- Decision support on org, roadmap, and priorities.
- AI opportunity framing.
- Executive communication support.

**Outcome statement:**
Get senior data and AI leadership judgment without over-hiring too early.

## 16. Conversion model

### Define conversion clearly
There is not one conversion. The website should support multiple intent levels.

### Conversion ladder
1. Read and understand the positioning.
2. Click into offers or credibility.
3. Click CTA.
4. Book discovery call or send inquiry.
5. Optionally subscribe to Data Biz.
6. Later: buy paid content or paid playbooks.

### Primary conversion event
Booked discovery call.

### Secondary conversion events
- CTA click.
- Contact form submit.
- Outbound click to Substack.
- Newsletter/subscription opt-in.

## 18. Design direction seed

### Desired feel
- Editorial.
- Executive.
- Modern Swiss.
- Warm but sharp.
- Minimal motion.
- High trust.

### Avoid
- Neon gradients.
- Futuristic AI clichés.
- Generic SaaS template look.
- “Agency bro” marketing style.
- Too much motion.
- Too many CTAs.

### Visual direction
- Warm neutral background.
- Dark text.
- One restrained teal or blue-green accent.
- Strong display serif or refined grotesk for headings.
- Clean sans-serif for body text.
- Left-aligned layouts with selective asymmetry.

## 19. Recommended font direction

Possible pairings:
- Boska + Satoshi.
- Zodiak + General Sans.
- Instrument Serif + Work Sans.

The typography should feel thoughtful and premium, not corporate-default.

## 21. Copy constraints

The copy should:
- Be blunt.
- Avoid fluffy claims.
- Avoid “empower”, “unlock”, “journey”, “solutions”.
- Sound like an operator talking to senior peers.
- Be short enough to scan fast.
- Still feel premium and thoughtful.

## 22. CTA strategy

### Main CTA
Book a discovery call.

### Secondary CTA
Read Data Biz.

### Tertiary CTA
Possible later: get the framework / download playbook.

The site should always make the commercial next step obvious.

## 23. Technical recommendation for POC

### Best fit
- Single static HTML file.
- Clean CSS or Tailwind.
- Minimal JavaScript.
- Calendly link or placeholder CTA.
- Analytics events wired in clearly.

### Not needed yet
- CMS.
- Authentication.
- Multi-page architecture.
- Client portal.
- Overbuilt animations.

## 24. Open questions to resolve before final version

- Should the brand stay as Data Biz long term, or later shift to a personal-name-led consultancy?
- Is the main buyer a data leader, or an executive buyer like COO/CFO/CEO?
- How much should AI appear in the headline versus in supporting copy?
- Which offer should be the hero offer?
- Should the CTA be “Book a call” or “Scope a workshop”?
- Will there be a lead magnet on day one?
- Will the website support both consulting and future employment branding?

## 25. First build checklist

Before coding:
- Pick one headline.
- Pick one subheadline.
- Pick the three launch offers.
- Pick three featured essays.
- Pick the main CTA.
- Pick one typography pairing.
- Pick one accent color.
- Decide whether founder identity is prominent or subtle.

During coding:
- Keep it one page.
- Keep one primary CTA visible.
- Make sections easy to reorder.
- Leave room to swap copy quickly.
- Build for desktop and mobile.

After shipping:
- Send traffic from LinkedIn and Substack.
- Add YouTube later as top funnel.
- Review CTA clicks and booked calls.
- Rewrite based on what visitors actually do.
